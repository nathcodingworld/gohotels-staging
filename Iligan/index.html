Sights to See in Iligan City

Below are a few points of interest located within Iligan City. Some of these locations can be reached by public transport or on foot from Go Hotels Iligan. There are also car rental and taxi services that can take you to any point in Iligan City as well as to adjoining municipalities. Please feel free to ask our hotel staff for directions and suggestions.

  • Waterfalls

    Iligan City is famous throughout the country for its collection of majestic waterfalls, with 24 major waterfalls located either within its city limits or just a short distance away. Of these, Maria Cristina Falls is probably the most well-known outside of Iligan. In addition to the city's famous waterfalls, there are dozens of streams and other freshwater features within a short distance from the hotel.
    Some of the most-visited falls include Maria Cristina, Tinago, Limunsudan, Mimbalut, Dodiongan, Kalubihon, and Pampam. Aside from the 24 major waterfalls, there are also other falls that are unnamed that are just outside city limits. You can ask the hotel staff the best way to get to any of the most popular falls and water features.    ...

    Hindang Cave

    This challenging spelunking spot is not for everyone. The uphill trail leading up to this spot is best tackled by experienced hikers. However, those who can complete the half-day trek can experience one of the best spelunking sites in the country. Hikers wishing to explore the cave will need to pay a small trekking fee at the barangay hall. For the safest experience, it’s important to take on the services of a qualified tour guide as well. ...

  • Iligan Public Plaza

    Located in Iligan’s historic downtown area, the public plaza is a popular site for recreational and social activities. It is also the site of a popular weekend night market. If you want to get a quick sense of Iligan’s people and culture, visiting the plaza should give you a good introduction. ...

    St. Michael’s Cathedral

    While relatively new by the standards of most Philippine cathedrals, the Cathedral of Saint Michael the Archangel is, nevertheless, a central part of the Iliganon identity. Each September, a statue of Saint Michael the Archangel that is usually set in a high alcove inside the cathedral is moved to a lower nook located outside, as part of the “Diyandi/Kasadya” festival....

  • Paseo de Santiago

    This seaside park is half a kilometer long and offers a great view of Iligan Bay. The park is a popular picnic spot and is a sanctuary for sea creatures endemic to this part of Mindanao. Loud activities are highly discouraged here, making it a favorite hangout spot for some Iliganons....

    NPC Nature’s Park

    This park is the site of the famous Maria Cristina Falls. It is named after the publicly-owned National Power Corporation, which runs the park and operates a hydroelectric power generation facility within its limits. The site also contains zoological and botanical gardens featuring a variety of native fauna and flora. There are also opportunities to see wild animals roaming free throughout the park....

  • Villa Gracia

    You no longer have to go to Cagayan De Oro to experience the unique thrill of navigating fast-moving river currents. The Villa Gracia resort is the first of its kind in Iligan to offer a true white water rafting experience. It is located in a quick-flowing upstream portion of the Mandulog River, just 15 kilometers away from downtown Iligan....

When Is the Best Time to Visit Iligan City?

Most Iliganons might suggest that you visit Iligan City in late September, as this is when the city’s Diyandi Festival is held. Also called the “Kasadya Festival”, Diyandi is a feast in honor of St. Michael the Archangel, the city’s patron. It is also a commemoration of the union of the Maranao and Higa-onon peoples who are native to the area.
Because of the large number of local tourists visiting the area during this time, visitors interested in observing the Diyandi Festival should book their hotel in Iligan City a few months in advance.
If you’re interested in enjoying Iligan’s many outdoor activities, you may want to avoid visiting during the hottest and wettest months. If the heat and rain are a concern, you may want to book hotel accommodations from mid-December to mid-April, as these are the driest, coolest times of the year.
