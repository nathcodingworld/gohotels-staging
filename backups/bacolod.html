When Is the Best Time to Visit Bacolod?

Most locals would agree that October would be the best time to visit Bacolod. This is because October is the month when the world-famous MassKara festival is observed.

During MassKara, various events such as food festivals, cultural shows, street dancing, street parties, and free concerts are held in the weeks leading up to the culmination of the festival on the last Sunday of October. The end of the festival coincides neatly with the school semestral break and the observance of All Saints’ and All Souls’ Day, both of which are special days for pious Catholic Filipinos.

If you're planning to visit Bacolod for MassKara, make sure to book your room at Go Hotels a few months in advance. MassKara attracts hundreds of thousands of tourists each year, so hotels in Bacolod are usually fully booked at this time, especially the ones located in ideal spots such as Go Hotels.

If you're not able to make the MassKara festival this year, don't worry. Bacolod has mild weather all year round, which makes it a nice place to visit at virtually any time of the year. The rainiest months tend to be from June to October and the hottest months are from March to April. The coolest and driest time of the year tends to be January, so schedule your trip around that time if you’re planning outdoor activities.

Sights to See around Bacolod City

Below are a few local sites that are within close proximity to Go Hotels Bacolod. Many of these locations can be reached by public transport or on foot. There are also taxi services that can take you anywhere in Bacolod for a reasonable fee. Please feel free to ask our staff for directions.

  • Negros Museum

    The Negros Museum's unique collection of artifacts gives you a glimpse of what life was like for the elite families of Negros during the late 19th and early 20th centuries. The museum also delves extensively into the culture surrounding sugar, the island’s main cash crop and a symbol of Negros. It also has an art gallery that features rotating collections of works from Negrense artists.

  • Capitol Park and Lagoon

    The Capitol Park and Lagoon (or simply the “Lagoon” as Bacolodnons call it) is a large park located in front of the Negros Occidental Provincial Capitol. The capitol building features neoclassical architecture similar to that of other American colonial-era buildings constructed in the early 20th century.

    The park features an enormous man-made fishpond as its focal point. This public space is well-appointed with benches, assembly areas, and spots for exercise and recreation.

    If you’re traveling with children or want a more relaxing diversion, you can buy puffed rice from surrounding vendors to feed the fish in the pond. If you’re in search of quality pasalubong the Negros Showroom is located just across Lacson Street, at the east side of the Lagoon.

  • Manokan Country

    Bacolod City is a food lover's paradise, and it may be impossible to sample every “must-try” item in a month, let alone a weekend. However, most locals would agree that Manokan Country is a place you shouldn’t miss.

    Located in Bacolod’s reclamation area, Manokan Country is a row of restaurants that serve chicken inasal, arguably the city’s signature dish. Each of the chicken inasal joints at Manokan Country has its own take on the dish, but you can’t go wrong with any of the restaurants located here.

  • Old Pala-Pala Market

    If fresh seafood is more your thing, Bacolod’s Old Pala-Pala Market won’t disappoint. For decades, this public market has been a go-to for quality-conscious chefs at the city’s hundreds of restaurants. But if you can’t cook, don’t worry — you can have your fresh seafood cooked just the way you like it by any of the dozens of cooks at the market.

  • Bacolod City Plaza and San Sebastian Cathedral

    The San Sebastian Cathedral is a symbol of Bacolod City and is a rare example of a 19th-century cathedral built in a Baroque style. It overlooks the Bacolod City Plaza, a popular rest spot for locals. The cathedral and plaza both contain markers for various historic events that happened in Bacolod and are culturally important for most native Bacolodnons.

  • Heritage Houses

    While The Ruins is rightfully one of the area’s most popular tourist attractions, there are dozens of other intact heritages houses dating back to the late 19th and early 20th centuries scattered all over Bacolod as well as in nearby Talisay (the site of The Ruins) and Silay. Many of these houses are in a remarkable state of preservation, and many are still inhabited by the descendants of the families that built them.

    Silay City has an especially remarkable collection of these well-maintained heritage houses, given the closeness of these structures. Given that Bacolod–Silay International Airport is only 30 minutes away from Go Hotels Bacolod, it’s a great idea to make these houses your last stop before departing Negros.

  • Negros Forests and Ecological Foundation Biodiversity Conservation Center

    Located next to the Lagoon, the Negros Forests and Ecological Foundation Biodiversity Conservation Center is a wildlife rescue and conservation center that houses native species, including some of the world’s rarest animals.

    The center is home to an impressive collection of endangered animals including several bird species, giant golden-crowned flying foxes (Acerodon jubatus), and Visayan leopard cats (Prionailurus javanensis sumatranus).

    Notably, it is home to breeding pairs of the world’s rarest deer, the Visayan spotted deer (Rusa alfredi), and the world’s rarest pig, the Negros subspecies of the Visayan warty pig (Sus cebifrons negrinus).
