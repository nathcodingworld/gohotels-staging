What to Do in Tuguegarao City

Many points of interest are easily reachable from Go Hotels Tuguegarao, whether you opt to take a car or public transport. In case you need help figuring out how to maximize your time in Tuguegarao City, don’t hesitate to ask our staff for directions or recommendations.
The following are just a few major activities you won’t want to miss during your visit to Tuguegarao:

  • See the Minor Basilica of Our Lady of Piat

    Devoted Catholics, history buffs, and lovers of art and architecture will all definitely want to visit the Minor Basilica of Our Lady of Piat, the largest Catholic church in Cagayan province. The church is located in the town of Piat, an approximate 30-minute drive northwest from Tuguegarao City. It houses a distinctive black image of the Virgin Mary and the infant Jesus which Dominican friars first brought to the Philippines from Macau in 1604. This image now draws pilgrims and devotees to Cagayan Valley from all over the country.
    The basilica boasts an imposing domed ceiling, an interior lined with dazzling stained-glass windows, and multiple alcoves in which visitors can gaze upon restored images of saints. While you don’t need to be a believer by any means to appreciate its majesty, don’t be surprised if you come away from your visit feeling like you’ve been in the presence of something divine.    ...

    Visit the Cagayan Museum and Historical Research Center

    If you’d like to learn more about the history and culture of Cagayan Valley, stop in at the Cagayan Museum and Historical Research Center. This fascinating facility houses multiple exhibits that comprises fossils, pieces of art, antiques, cultural artifacts, and other ethnographic items. Simply perusing these exhibits will no doubt uncover a wealth of stories to you about Cagayan and its people....

  • Explore Callao and Sierra Caves

    Callao Cave is probably Cagayan Valley’s most iconic tourist attraction. Once you explore its seven chambers, you’ll find yourself dazzled by how these typically darkened spaces can be brilliantly illuminated by the light coming in through holes in the cave roof. The highlight of the experience is a beautiful subterranean chapel, where you can say a prayer or simply sit in silence for a while, admiring the majesty that surrounds you.
    It's entirely possible to make your way through Callao Cave on foot, and the abundant light in the vicinity makes exploring it an easy journey as long as you’re careful. More seasoned spelunkers in search of a bigger challenge will want to head to the nearby Sierra Caves, which are less likely to be crowded with tourists. Be prepared for an adventure that may leave you muddy from head to toe as you crawl through the caves’ many narrow openings.    ...

    Cross the Buntun Bridge

    San Francisco’s Golden Gate Bridge has a worthy rival in Tuguegarao City’s Buntun Bridge, one of the longest bridges in the Philippines. This 1.37-kilometer bridge stretches across the Cagayan River and makes for a wonderfully scenic drive on a clear day. The view may even inspire you to put on your walking shoes and traverse it on foot for a daily exercise experience unlike any other. ...

  • Sample Local Delicacies

    When in Tuguegarao, you’ll want to eat like a local. Food items made from carabao products are popular in the city, such as carabao milk pastillas and chicharabao, a chicharron-like snack made of carabao skin. Don’t miss the fabled Pancit Batil Patong, one of Tuguegarao’s signature dishes. This noodle-based dish incorporates carabao beef, bean sprouts, pork liver, egg, and crumbled chicharron and is topped with a special sauce. It won’t be difficult to get hold of a good hearty serving of Pancit Batil Patong, either, as many local eateries will offer it as a house specialty....

Go Hotels Plus Tuguegarao

The city of Tuguegarao is located on a sprawling plain surrounding the Cagayan River and serves as the provincial capital of Cagayan Valley. Founded in 1604 as a “mission-pueblo” by priests of the Dominican religious order, the city has grown over the years into one of northern Luzon’s foremost urban centers. As the only urbanized city in Cagayan province, Tuguegarao functions as the primary transportation hub for the rest of the surrounding region.
Tuguegarao’s atmosphere is a charming blend of urban and provincial, similarly to many other smaller cities located outside of urban centers. The ever-reliable tricycle and the horse-drawn karitela remain the most common types of public transport that both locals and visitors use to get around. On the other hand, there’s also no shortage of hospitals, hotels, banks, restaurants, and other businesses here that you’d find in any contemporary city.
